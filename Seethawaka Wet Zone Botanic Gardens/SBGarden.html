Seethawaka Wet Zone Botanic Gardens
This 105 acre beautiful botanic garden is based in Avissawella. This garden remains mostly as a research and conservation area for threatened species of endemic plants. Some of their goals is to export flower populations and also cultivate bamboo. There are a vast variety of trees and flowers which is supported by a large lake, perfect for wet zone plants.
Seethawaka's gorgeous flowers
Beautifully shaped little red flowers. They kind of reminded me of lavendar. They are just so vibrant.
These are unique flowers, with colour combinations I hadn't seen before. I especially love the brown buds at the top.
I am a sucker for red roses. These roses were perfect and so plump.
Sensational scenery views
This was a mini hedge and bush area. We were twice the height of these cute bushes.

The man-made walking paths complimented the nature due to its subtleness. I loved walking through this area.
Refreshing waterfalls
This was the only picture taken in Seethawaka. The mini waterfalls are just so cute.
Next to Seethawaka was a small remote waterfall. We walked in the water up this stream. It was refreshing and so pleasant. Some people were also sitting and bathing with the stream pouring over their shoulders.
We didn't want to get too close to the edge as the stream was quick and strong, but it is a magnificent view and there were people bathing under the waterfall of this ledge.
